Frequently Asked Questions

Key Terms Explained

Borrowing Power
The estimated maximum amount a lender may be willing to loan you based on your income, expenses, and other financial factors.
Loan to Value Ratio (LVR)
The ratio of the loan amount compared to the value of the property. For example, an 80% LVR means you borrow 80% of the property's price.
Loan Mortgage Insurance (LMI)
A one-off insurance payment usually required when the deposit is less than 20% of the property value, protecting the lender against default.
Stamp Duty
A state government tax paid when purchasing property. Rates and thresholds vary by state.
Monthly Repayment
The estimated amount you will pay monthly to repay your home loan, including principal and interest.

Common Questions

Is this website providing financial advice?
No. This site offers tools and calculators to help you estimate borrowing power and loan repayments based on your inputs. It does not replace professional financial or legal advice.
How accurate are these calculators?
These calculators provide estimates based on typical rates and simplified assumptions. Actual lending decisions depend on your full financial situation and lender policies.
Why do I need to enter my expenses?
Your monthly expenses impact your ability to service a loan. Including them helps estimate a realistic borrowing power and repayment capacity.
What if I have a deposit less than 20%?
If your deposit is less than 20% of the property price, Loan Mortgage Insurance (LMI) may apply, which is included in the estimates.
